2. Personal Data We Process

  • Identity & contact details (name, email, phone, address, date of birth)
  • Booking & treatment information (history, preferences, patch-test notes)
  • Payment records (processed via secure payment providers; we don’t store full card details)
  • Communications (emails, forms, messages)
  • Technical data (IP, device, cookies/analytics as explained below)
  • Photos/consent forms where applicable (e.g., before & after with your explicit consent)

3. Lawful Bases

We rely on one or more of the following lawful bases:

  • Consent (e.g., marketing emails, photos)
  • Contract (to provide treatments or manage bookings)
  • Legal obligation (e.g., tax, health & safety)
  • Legitimate interests (e.g., clinic security, service improvement)
  • Vital interests (rare cases relating to safety)

4. Purposes of Processing

  • Booking management, treatment delivery, and client support
  • Payment processing and fraud prevention
  • Service personalisation and reminders
  • Safety, audit, and legal compliance
  • Marketing with your consent and subject to your preferences

5. Data Retention

We keep personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes above and to meet legal, tax, and insurance requirements. Typical retention: treatment/consent records up to 7 years; marketing data until you withdraw consent; operational logs for shorter periods. When data is no longer needed, we securely delete or anonymise it.

6. Sharing & Processors

We may share data with trusted service providers acting under our instructions (e.g., booking software, email, cloud hosting, payments). We require processors to implement appropriate security and never use your data for their own purposes.

We may also share data where required by law, to protect rights/safety, or with your consent.

7. International Transfers

If personal data is transferred outside the UK, we ensure appropriate safeguards (e.g., UK IDTA / SCCs and vendor security measures) so your rights remain protected.

8. Your Rights

  • Access your data and receive a copy
  • Rectify incomplete or inaccurate data
  • Erase data (where applicable)
  • Restrict or object to processing (including direct marketing)
  • Data portability
  • Withdraw consent at any time (where processing is based on consent)

9. How to Exercise Your Rights

Email {{emailText}} with your request. We may ask for information to verify your identity. We aim to respond within one month as required by law.

10. Cookies & Similar Technologies

We use essential cookies to operate the site and (where enabled) analytics/marketing cookies to improve the experience. You can manage preferences via your browser and our cookie banner. See our Privacy Policy for details.

11. Marketing

We send marketing only with your consent or as otherwise permitted by law. You can unsubscribe anytime via the link in our emails or by contacting us.

12. Automated Decision-Making

We do not carry out automated decision-making that produces legal or similarly significant effects. If this ever changes, we will inform you and explain your rights.

13. Children

Our Services are intended for adults. Where treatments are provided to minors, we obtain appropriate consent and treat data with enhanced care.

14. Complaints

If you have concerns about our use of your personal data, please contact us first. You also have the right to complain to the UK Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO): ico.org.uk.
